About Us

Rescue, Rehabilitate, Release

ACT Wildlife is the national capitals only charity organisation dedicated to helping our native wildlife thrive, through good and bad times. Our dedicated team of volunteers rescue and care for injured or orphaned animals until they are well enough to be released into the wild.

We also provide educational programs for members of public, teaching them how better share their city with its native wildlife, premoting a more harmonious existence. In addition to this are our proffessional training courses, which equip members with the skills to care for animals at the highest standard.

Our Projects

Wombat Mange Project

Currently, ACT Wildlife is teaming up with members of the public to help reduce instances of mange in local wombats.

Mange is a very painful and debilitating skin condition often seen in wombats that is caused by parasitic mites. These mites bury into the wombats skin and can cause itching, hair loss, wounds, and infections that can lead to death. Thankfully, these mites can be killed with medicinal treatment, which needs to be applied to the skin for regularly over a 16 week duration.

ACT Wildlife has secured funding for these treatments and can provide them free to any member of the public willing and able to help our wombats out.
